ABOUT THE SHOW

Cyberpunk: Edgerunners tells a standalone, 10-episode story about a street

kid trying to survive in a technology and body modification-obsessed city of

the future.

Having everything to lose, he chooses to stay alive by becoming an

edgerunner—a mercenary outlaw also known as a cyberpunk.

WHAT'S NEW

CD PROJEKT RED and animation studio TRIGGER are returning to Night City with an entirely new story, fresh characters, and a raw, realistic take on the most dangerous city of the dark future. At the helm of Cyberpunk: Edgerunners 2 is director Kai Ikarashi, lead character designer Kanno Ichigo, and the creative minds behind Cyberpunk: Edgerunner's story: Hugo Award winner Bartosz Sztybor and Masahiko Otsuka.

MANGA

Cyberpunk: Edgerunners MADNESS, the first-ever manga set in the world of the hit anime, tells the story of siblings Rebecca and Pilar before they joined David’s crew. The duo follows in their father’s footsteps to become legendary edgerunners — and wreak havoc along the way. Join the chaos with the upcoming paperback release from Dark Horse Comics.

STORY

In the aftermath of a personal tragedy, David Martinez chooses to take control of his own life by becoming an edgerunner and entering the criminal underworld of Night City. Cyberpunk: Edgerunners asks just how far you can go to overcome your physical and emotional limits, and what the pursuit of fame within a city ruled by greed and disrespect for human life can lead to.

CHARACTERS

A cast of memorable characters fill Night City in Cyberpunk: Edgerunners, each ruthlessly pushing their own goals and agendas. Whether you root for their success or their downfall, none will leave you indifferent to their loss. As their paths intertwine, the weight of their choices will linger, leaving a lasting impact on those who dare to follow their story.

CREATORS

Cyberpunk: Edgerunners is an award-winning Netflix anime created in collaboration between CD PROJEKT RED and the legendary Japanese animation studio TRIGGER. Known for critically acclaimed TV series, including Little Witch Academia and Delicious in Dungeon, as well as the movies Promare and Gridman Universe, brought Night City to life thanks to an exquisite creator team and cast.
